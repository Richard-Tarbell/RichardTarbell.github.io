About Me

Undergrad

Orginally an accounting major freshman year I took one accounting course and knew it wasn't the career path for me. Not sure where to go next that following summer I decided to pursue my interest in Astronomy and made the drastic switch to Physics. While it was a challenging 4 more years (I took a victory lap) I wouldn't have it any other way!

While in my undergraduate I became heavily involved with the physics department and the organizations within it. Eventually becoming the President of the Society of Physics Students (SPS) from Spring 2019 all the way up until graduation in Spring 2021. While I was President our chapter recieved the Outstanding Chapter Award which is the highest distinction given to chapters and is received by less than 15% of the top chapters annually.

Early on in my physics degree I had the privelege of meeting the observatory manager at UTSA, Bryan Tobias. Bryan and I quickly became close and he entrusted me with setting up the telescopes and assisting during outreach events that the department hosted. This only helped fuel my love for astronomy and I found myself using the telescopes by myself every chance I got (weather permitting). It was during my time volunteering with the observatory I Co-Founded the Astronomy Club along another volunteer Greg.

Graduate School

Why Grad School?

As I became involved with research during undergrad I had to face the harsh reality that a life of astronomy research wasn't for me. While I do have love for the field 5+ more years of school for a PhD was out of the picture (mostly to prevent burnout). However I still have a heavy interest in computational problem solving and working with all kinds of data. I began looking for traditional career paths for physics and the choices were slim. It seemed I either was going to end up in a lab or I need to batten down the hatches and really expand my computational skillset. Naturally I looked at towards the field of Data Science, with the complex problem solving and the collaboration I knew it was the perfect fit for me. With this knowledge and desire to pursue my new plan I started looking at the Master of Science in Data Analytics program at UTSA. With the program offering a daytime cohort option allowing to finish in one calendar year along with professors and courses ranging from cyber security to data ethics it was an easy decision.

Courses

While I've only been in the program for one semester (Fall 2021) I've gained a ton of knowledge and insight to the world of Data Analytics. The were first courses I took were:

  • IS 6713 - Data Foundations
  • STA 6443 - Data Analytics Algorithms I
  • DA 6223 - Data Analytics Tools and Techniques
  • DA 6233 - Data Analytics Visualization and Communication
  • MKT 6971 - Special Problems (Data Analytics Practicum I part 1)
